Traffic Project

by Stephen McClure

I went into this project with the mindset of answering a question I've had for years. Are people more dangerous drivers now since I started driving? The idea would be to find data I can use to answer this question. I would start by finding data on accidents since those mostly are reported.

The Data

What I found is a summary of the US total reported traffic accidents by quarter going back to 2007.

Source of quarterly data from NHTSA.

After getting the data, I cleaned the table to focus on just the quarter totals and then graphed them:

Interestingly enough visualising the data this way I noticed two things. Firstly that traffic incidents have leveled out since 2007 and while there has been a short climb since I would say this doesn't show enough for me to definitively say people are more dangerous drivers. In fact the second thing I noticed is that the incidents climb for each summer. With the exception of 2014 each year is a pyramid peaking in summer of accidents. What this may suggest to me is that maybe instead of people being more dangerous I just noticed it more because I probably follow the same trend and drive more in the summer. This is also fascinating because despite road conditions being worse in the winter it is the lowest accounting of incidents each year. Although I have to bear in mind this data likely only represents reported incidents.
